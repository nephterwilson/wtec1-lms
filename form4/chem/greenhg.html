What is the Ozone Layer?

The ozone layer is a region in the Earth's stratosphere, located approximately 20 to 30 km above the surface, that absorbs most of the sun's harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation.

Importance of the Ozone Layer

‚òÄÔ∏è

Protection Against UV Rays

The ozone layer absorbs 93% to 99% of UV light, which can be harmful to living organisms.

Depletion of the Ozone Layer

Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) are the primary agents responsible for ozone layer depletion.

Common sources of CFCs include coolants, foams, and aerosol products.
