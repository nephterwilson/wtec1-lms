Isomers vs Conformers

Isomers have different structural formulae and names; conformers differ only in shape due to bond rotation.

üîó

Chain Isomerism

Different arrangement of carbon atoms in the skeleton - all organic compounds can show this.

üìç

Position Isomerism

Different position of functional group - only possible when functional group can move (alkenes, alkanols, alkanones).

‚ö†Ô∏è

Fixed Functional Groups

Alkanals and alkanoic acids have functional groups fixed at the end - no position isomerism possible.

üî¢

Numbering Rules

Always number the parent chain to give the functional group or first branch the lowest possible number.

üìù

Systematic Naming

Use prefixes (di-, tri-, tetra-) for multiple identical groups and always state position numbers.
