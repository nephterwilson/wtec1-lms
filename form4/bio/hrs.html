What is Breathing?

Breathing Process

Breathing involves the intake and expulsion of air from the lungs, allowing oxygen to enter the body and carbon dioxide to be removed.

This vital process ensures our cells receive the oxygen needed for energy production through cellular respiration.

Control Center

The medulla oblongata in the brain regulates the basic rhythm of breathing and controls the diaphragm and intercostal muscles.

This ensures automatic breathing without conscious effort, adjusting to our body's needs.

Role of Haemoglobin

Haemoglobin is a protein found in red blood cells with a high affinity for oxygen.

It picks up oxygen in the lungs and delivers it to tissue cells throughout the body, then returns to pick up more oxygen.

Investigating Exercise Effects on Breathing

Compare breathing rate before and after physical activity

üéØ AIM

To measure and compare the breathing rate before and after physical activity

üß∞ MATERIALS

üìã PROCEDURE

Part 1: Resting Measurement
  1. Sit calmly: Sit comfortably in a chair or seat in a quiet area
  2. Rest period: Ensure you are at rest and calm for at least 5 minutes
  3. Measure: Use the stopwatch to measure the number of breaths (inhalation AND exhalation count as ONE breath) for one minute
  4. Record: Write down the number as the resting breathing rate
Part 2: Physical Activity
  1. Stand up: Prepare to run in place in a clear space
  2. Exercise: Run in place for 5 minutes at a moderate pace
  3. Maintain pace: Keep the activity consistent throughout
Part 3: Post-Exercise Measurement
  1. Sit immediately: After running, return to the chair and sit down right away
  2. Brief recovery: Allow yourself to rest for at least 1 minute
  3. Measure again: Count the number of breaths for one minute using the stopwatch
  4. Record: Write down this number as the post-exercise breathing rate
Part 4: Analysis
  1. Compare rates: Compare the resting breathing rate with the post-exercise rate
  2. Analyze results: Observe and note the difference
  3. Draw conclusions: Explain why the breathing rate changed
