What is Asthma?

Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition that causes the bronchi and bronchioles to constrict, leading to significant difficulty in breathing. The airways become narrowed, making it hard for air to flow in and out of the lungs.

üí® Shortness of Breath

üò∞ Chest Tightness

Bronchitis

ü§ß

What is Bronchitis?

Bronchitis is an inflammation of the bronchial tubes that carry air to and from the lungs. This condition leads to swelling and irritation of the airways, making breathing difficult.

ü¶† Causes & Mechanism

Primary Cause

Mucus buildup in the bronchial tubes damages the delicate cilia (tiny hair-like structures)

Progressive Damage

The damaged cilia cannot effectively clear mucus, leading to more accumulation and persistent irritation

Result

Airways become inflamed and narrowed, triggering persistent coughing as the body tries to clear the obstruction

ü§ß Persistent Cough

Chronic coughing that lasts for weeks or months, often worse in the morning

üíß Mucus Production

Production of thick, discolored mucus (white, yellow, or green) that is coughed up

üòÆ‚Äçüí® Breathing Difficulty

Shortness of breath and wheezing, especially during physical activity

ü§í Chest Discomfort

Chest tightness and discomfort from constant coughing

‚è±Ô∏è Types of Bronchitis

Acute Bronchitis

Short-term inflammation lasting 1-3 weeks, often following a cold or flu

Chronic Bronchitis

Long-term condition lasting months or years, often caused by smoking or air pollution

  • ‚Ä¢ Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke
  • ‚Ä¢ Stay away from air pollutants
  • ‚Ä¢ Wash hands frequently to prevent infections
  • ‚Ä¢ Stay hydrated to thin mucus
  • ‚Ä¢ Use a humidifier to moisten air
  • ‚Ä¢ Get adequate rest during recovery

Pulmonary Tuberculosis (TB)

ü¶†

What is Tuberculosis?

Tuberculosis (TB) is a serious bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs but can spread to other organs including the brain, spine, and kidneys. It is one of the world's deadliest infectious diseases.

üî¨ Cause & Transmission

Causative Agent

Caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis, a slow-growing organism

How It Spreads

Transmitted through airborne droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, speaks, or spits

Contagious Nature

Highly contagious when in close contact with infected individuals in poorly ventilated spaces

üë∂

ü©∫

üíä

üéóÔ∏è

What is Lung Cancer?

Lung cancer occurs when tumors form in the alveoli (air sacs) due to abnormal cell mitosis. These cancerous cells divide uncontrollably, forming masses that interfere with normal lung function.

üö¨ Primary Cause

Tobacco Smoke

The leading cause of lung cancer. Tar from tobacco smoke contains carcinogens that stimulate abnormal cell growth

How Tar Damages Lungs

Tar deposits in alveoli, damaging DNA and triggering uncontrolled cell division

  • ‚Ä¢ Secondhand smoke exposure
  • ‚Ä¢ Radon gas
  • ‚Ä¢ Asbestos and air pollution
  • ‚Ä¢ Family history

‚ò†Ô∏è

What is CO Poisoning?

Carbon monoxide poisoning is caused by inhalation of carbon monoxide (CO), a colorless, odorless, and tasteless poisonous gas. It's called the "silent killer" because you can't see, taste, or smell it.

üî• Common Sources

ü™µ

üöó

Running Engines

üè†

üî•

Other Combustion

Kerosene heaters, gas appliances

‚öóÔ∏è How CO Affects the Body

Formation of Carboxyhemoglobin

CO binds to hemoglobin 200 times more strongly than oxygen, forming carboxyhemoglobin

Oxygen Blockage

Prevents oxygen from dissociating easily and being delivered to tissues

Reduced Oxygen Supply

Tissues, including the brain, receive insufficient oxygen, leading to cell damage and death
