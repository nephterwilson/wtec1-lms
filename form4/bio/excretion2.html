Human Excretory System

Understanding how the body removes metabolic waste

Excretory System Overview

Excretion is the process by which organisms remove waste products of metabolism from their bodies.

Metabolism refers to all chemical reactions occurring in cells.

  • CO₂ from respiration
  • Excess water
  • Urea, uric acid, ammonia
  • Drug and hormone by-products
  • Bile pigments

Organs & Functions

  • Skin: removes water & salts as sweat
  • Lungs: remove CO₂ & water vapour
  • Liver: removes bile pigments & toxins
  • Kidneys: remove nitrogenous wastes & excess salts/water

Urinary System Components

  • Kidneys – produce urine
  • Ureters – carry urine to bladder
  • Bladder – stores urine
  • Urethra – passes urine out of body

Kidney Structure

  • Cortex – outer region
  • Medulla – inner region
  • Pelvis – collects urine
  • Nephrons – functional units
  • Renal artery/vein – blood supply

Nephron Function

  • Afferent arteriole → glomerulus → efferent arteriole
  • Bowman's capsule: ultrafiltration
  • PCT: reabsorbs useful substances
  • Loop of Henle: concentrates urine
  • DCT: ion & pH balance
  • Collecting duct: final water reabsorption

Urine Formation

  1. Ultrafiltration – in Bowman's capsule
  2. Selective reabsorption – in PCT, Loop, DCT
  3. Secretion – wastes added in DCT & collecting duct

Diet & Kidney Health

  • Drink enough water
  • Limit salt intake
  • Eat fruits & vegetables
  • Avoid excess protein
  • Avoid carbonated drinks & alcohol
