Key Difference

The main distinction between aerobic and anaerobic respiration is the presence or absence of oxygen. Aerobic respiration is far more efficient, producing significantly more ATP per glucose molecule. However, anaerobic respiration serves as a crucial backup system when oxygen is unavailable or insufficient for cellular needs.

Lower Energy Production

Anaerobic respiration produces significantly less energy compared to aerobic respiration. While aerobic respiration yields approximately 38 ATP molecules per glucose molecule, anaerobic respiration only produces 2 ATP molecules.

This inefficiency occurs because glucose is not completely broken down. The end products (ethanol or lactic acid) still contain considerable chemical energy that remains locked and unavailable to the cell.
