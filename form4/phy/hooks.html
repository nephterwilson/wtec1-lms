What is Hooke's Law?

Hooke's Law describes the relationship between the force applied to an elastic object and the extension produced. It is a fundamental concept in elasticity and mechanics.

Named after Robert Hooke

17th-century physicist

Applies to springs

and other elastic materials

Linear relationship

within elastic limit

Definition and Formula

Hooke's Law states that the extension of an elastic material is directly proportional to the force applied, provided the elastic limit is not exceeded.

F = kx

F

Force applied

(Newtons, N)

k

Spring constant

(N/m)

x

Extension/compression

(meters, m)

Materials behave differently under force depending on the magnitude of the applied force.

Worked Example

Problem:

A spring stretches by 0.05 m when a force of 10 N is applied. Find the spring constant.

Using F = kx ‚áí k = F / x

k = 10 N / 0.05 m = 200 N/m

Answer: Spring constant is 200 N/m
