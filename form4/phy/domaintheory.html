Electromagnetism & Domain Theory

Electromagnetic Domain Theory

Inside a magnet, there are small regions where all atomic magnetic directions are aligned. These regions are called domains. Within each domain, the alignment is uniform.

Electromagnetism

Electromagnetism is the phenomenon where a metallic object acquires magnetic properties when an electric current flows through it. The object loses magnetism when the current stops.

Electromagnets

Electromagnets are temporary magnets created by placing a ferrous material (e.g., soft iron) inside a solenoid with direct current. They magnetize when the current is on and demagnetize when off.

Pattern: The magnetic field depends on the current flow through the solenoid.

Field Around Current-Carrying Conductors

  • Straight Wire: Iron filings form concentric circles; field strength decreases with distance. (Illustration placeholder)
  • Circular Loop: Filings arrange in a circular pattern; direction given by right-hand grip rule. (Illustration placeholder)
  • Solenoid: Magnetic field is stronger than a single loop, proportional to current and turns per unit length. (Illustration placeholder)

Fleming's Rules

  • Right-Hand Rule: Thumb → motion, First finger → magnetic field, Second finger → current. (Illustration placeholder)
  • Left-Hand Rule: Thumb → force, First finger → field, Second finger → current. (Illustration placeholder)

Electromagnetic Induction

Electromagnetic induction occurs when a conductor moves within a magnetic field, inducing current. Methods include moving a conductor or moving a magnet within a stationary conductor.

Faraday's Laws: EMF induced is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux.

Lenz's Law: Induced current opposes the change in magnetic field producing it.

Generators

  • AC Generator: Rotating coil in a magnetic field produces alternating current.
  • DC Generator: Similar to AC but uses a commutator to produce direct current.

Transformers

  • Step-Up Transformer: Increases voltage. Formula: Vs/Vp = Ns/Np
  • Step-Down Transformer: Decreases voltage. Formula applies similarly with fewer secondary turns.
  • Transformers work only with AC.
  • Efficiency less than 100% due to resistance, leakage flux, and core losses.

Power Transmission & Environmental Impact

  • Power losses occur due to resistance and leakage; minimized by high voltage, low current transmission.
  • Environmental impacts include CO₂ emissions, high water usage, radioactive leaks, and ecosystem disruption.
