What is Circular Motion?

Circular motion occurs when an object moves along a circular path. If the speed remains constant throughout the motion, it is called uniform circular motion.

Circular Motion

Uniform Circular Motion

Movement at constant speed along a circular path, though direction continuously changes.

Key Feature: Velocity changes due to direction change, even if speed is constant.

Centripetal Force

The Inward Force

Centripetal force is the inward force required to keep an object moving in a circular path.

Without this force, the object would move in a straight line (Newton's First Law).
