How to Use Fleming's Left-Hand Rule

Hold your left hand with the thumb, first finger, and second finger at right angles to each other. Point the first finger in the direction of the magnetic field (N to S), the second finger in the direction of current, and your thumb will point in the direction of motion (force).

Understanding Magnetism at the Atomic Level

The domain theory explains magnetism at the atomic level. It states that magnetic materials contain tiny regions called domains.

üîπ

What is a Domain?

A domain is a small region in which atomic magnets (dipoles) align in the same direction.

üîÑ

Random vs Aligned

In an unmagnetized material, domains are randomly arranged. When magnetized, they align in the same direction.
