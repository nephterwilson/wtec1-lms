Human Interaction with the Environment

How human activities affect and shape the natural world

Overview

Humans manipulate their environment to meet their needs ‚Äî unlike most organisms that adapt naturally to theirs. These activities have both positive and negative effects.

Common Human Activities

  • Cutting trees for settlement, agriculture, and infrastructure.
  • Using fertilizers and pesticides in farming.
  • Improper disposal of industrial, domestic, and plastic wastes.
  • Expansion of urban areas and industrialization.

Main Environmental Impacts

  • Land degradation and soil erosion.
  • Air, water, and soil pollution.
  • Global warming and climate change.
  • Loss of biodiversity through species extinction.

Land Degradation

üåæ

Land degradation is the decline in land productivity, making it less capable of supporting plants and animals.

Causes

  • Deforestation and overgrazing.
  • Poor farming methods like over-cultivation.
  • Mining and quarrying that destroy soil layers.
  • Soil erosion washing away nutrient-rich topsoil.

Human Factors Behind Land Degradation

  • Poverty: People depend on cutting trees for charcoal and income.
  • Illiteracy: Lack of awareness about environmental conservation.
  • Overpopulation: High demand for land and resources.
  • Negative Attitudes: People expect government to handle all environmental issues.

Pollution is the introduction of harmful substances or energy into the environment, causing damage to living organisms and ecosystems.

Air Pollution

  • Caused by emissions from factories, vehicles, and burning fossil fuels.
  • Releases gases like CO‚ÇÇ, SO‚ÇÇ, NOx, and hydrocarbons.

Effects

  • Causes acid rain and damages crops and buildings.
  • Leads to respiratory diseases like bronchitis.
  • Contributes to global warming and ozone depletion.

Control

  • Planting trees to absorb carbon dioxide.
  • Using renewable energy sources like solar and biogas.

Water Pollution

  • Caused by sewage, industrial waste, and oil spills entering water bodies.
  • Leads to oxygen depletion, eutrophication, and aquatic deaths.

Control

  • Treat sewage before discharge into rivers and lakes.
  • Use organic manure and reduce chemical fertilizers.

Soil Pollution

  • Caused by chemical pesticides, fertilizers, and poor waste disposal.
  • Degrades soil quality and harms beneficial organisms.

Control

  • Use biodegradable and eco-friendly pesticides.
  • Recycle and reuse non-biodegradable waste.
  • Practice crop rotation and biological pest control.

Climate Change

Climate change is the long-term alteration of temperature and weather patterns caused by human actions such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation.

Major Effects

  • Floods from heavy rainfall and rising sea levels.
  • Droughts from prolonged dry seasons and higher temperatures.
  • Stormy winds damaging crops and structures.
  • Melting polar ice leading to habitat loss.

Mitigation Measures

  • Reafforestation to absorb carbon dioxide and prevent erosion.
  • Using renewable energy sources instead of fossil fuels.
  • Growing drought-resistant and high-yield crop varieties.
