Ecologists use sampling to estimate population sizes without examining every individual. Large samples give more accurate estimates than small samples.

Why Food Webs Matter

Food webs are more realistic than simple food chains because they show the complexity of ecosystems. Most organisms eat multiple types of food and are eaten by multiple predators. Understanding food webs helps us predict how changes to one species might affect the entire ecosystem.

Energy Flow in Ecosystems

Energy flows through an ecosystem starting from the sun. Producers capture sunlight and convert it into chemical energy through photosynthesis. Consumers feed on producers and other consumers, transferring energy up the food chain.

However, energy is lost at each trophic level through respiration, excretion, and heat. This is why food chains rarely have more than 4-5 levels‚Äîthere simply isn't enough energy left to support organisms at higher levels. Decomposers play a crucial role by breaking down dead organisms and recycling nutrients back into the ecosystem.
