How a Potometer Works

Measuring Water Uptake to Estimate Transpiration Rate

Introduction to the Potometer

A potometer is an apparatus used to measure the rate of water uptake by a leafy shoot, which indirectly estimates the rate of transpiration. As water evaporates from the leaves, more water is drawn up through the stem, moving an air bubble through a capillary tube.

  • ‚Ä¢ Measures water uptake rather than direct transpiration.
  • ‚Ä¢ Used to study effects of humidity, light, wind, and temperature on transpiration.
  • ‚Ä¢ Movement of the air bubble provides a visible measure of water uptake rate.

Working Principle of the Potometer

When transpiration occurs, water loss from leaves creates a negative pressure in the xylem, drawing water upwards. In the potometer, this water movement moves an air bubble through a capillary tube, showing how fast water is taken up.

  • ‚Ä¢ Evaporation creates suction, pulling water from the reservoir through the stem.
  • ‚Ä¢ The air bubble‚Äôs movement in the capillary tube shows the volume of water absorbed.
  • ‚Ä¢ Distance moved per unit time gives an estimate of transpiration rate.

Using a Potometer ‚Äì Procedure

  1. Cut the leafy shoot under water to prevent air from entering xylem vessels.
  2. Insert the shoot into the potometer and seal tightly with petroleum jelly or rubber stopper.
  3. Fill the apparatus with water, leaving only one air bubble for tracking.
  4. Introduce the air bubble into the capillary tube and start the timer.
  5. Record the distance travelled by the bubble in a fixed time to calculate water uptake rate.

Precautions When Using a Potometer

  • ‚Ä¢ Ensure all connections are airtight to prevent false readings.
  • ‚Ä¢ Avoid touching leaves to prevent stomatal closure.
  • ‚Ä¢ Keep environmental conditions constant unless being tested.
  • ‚Ä¢ Use shoots of the same species and leaf size for consistency.

Limitations of a Potometer

  • ‚Ä¢ Measures water uptake, not actual water loss through transpiration.
  • ‚Ä¢ Some absorbed water is used in photosynthesis or stored in tissues.
  • ‚Ä¢ Air leaks or poor sealing reduce accuracy of results.
  • ‚Ä¢ Difficult to standardize stomatal opening and physiological state.
