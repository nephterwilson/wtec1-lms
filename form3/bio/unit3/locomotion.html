Fish Locomotion

Movement in water

Structures Used:

  • ‚Ä¢ Fins: Essential for movement
  • ‚Ä¢ Swim Bladder: Buoyancy control
  • ‚Ä¢ Block Muscles: Provide propulsion
  • ‚Ä¢ Tail: Powerful thrust

  • ‚Ä¢ Yawing (sideways): Median fins
  • ‚Ä¢ Rolling (tilting): Paired fins
  • ‚Ä¢ Pitching (up/down): Pectoral fins

Flight mechanisms

Types of Feathers:

  • ‚Ä¢ Flight Feathers: Broad, flat; generate lift
  • ‚Ä¢ Down Feathers: Insulation & heat retention
  • ‚Ä¢ Contour Feathers: Shape & coloration
  • ‚Ä¢ Filoplume: Sensory function

  • ‚Ä¢ Flapping: Downstroke (lift) & upstroke (recovery)
  • ‚Ä¢ Gliding: Wings spread, slow descent
  • ‚Ä¢ Soaring: Using air currents for height

ü¶Å

Mammal Locomotion

Movement on land, air, and water

Limb Types:

  • ‚Ä¢ Legs: Walking, running, jumping
  • ‚Ä¢ Wings (bats): Flying
  • ‚Ä¢ Flippers (whales): Swimming

  • ‚Ä¢ Bones: Support & structure
  • ‚Ä¢ Muscles: Generate movement
  • ‚Ä¢ Tendons: Connect muscles to bones
  • ‚Ä¢ Ligaments: Connect bones at joints

Movement Adaptations:
