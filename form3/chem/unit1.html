What is Scientific Investigation?

A scientific investigation is a systematic method used to answer questions about natural phenomena. It involves careful planning and observation.

1

Problem Identification

Asking questions about the natural world (e.g., causes of rusting)

2

Formulation of a Hypothesis

Guessed answer based on knowledge

3

Experimentation

Series of investigations to test the hypothesis

4

Observation and Data Collection

Accurate recording of observations and measurements

5

Interpretation of Data

Explaining observations and trends

6

Conclusion

Confirmation or rejection of the hypothesis; if rejected, formulate a new one

What is a Pure Substance?

A material with constant composition and no impurities.

Pure substances have constant composition and properties. Impurities alter physical characteristics like melting and boiling points.

Experiment 3: Paper Chromatography

Separating mixture components to test purity

üéØ Aim

To separate the components of a mixture and test for purity using paper chromatography

üß∞ Materials

  • ‚Ä¢ Chromatography paper
  • ‚Ä¢ Solvent (e.g., water or ethanol)
  • ‚Ä¢ Pencil
  • ‚Ä¢ Ruler
  • ‚Ä¢ Sample of the substance
  • ‚Ä¢ Sample of pure substance (if available)

üìã Procedure

  1. 1. Draw a baseline about 1 cm from the bottom of the chromatography paper using a pencil
  2. 2. Spot the sample on the baseline
  3. 3. If available, place a spot of pure substance next to it
  4. 4. Dip the bottom of the paper in solvent without touching the spots
  5. 5. Allow the solvent to rise and separate the components
  6. 6. Remove the paper when the solvent has moved about 3/4 up and mark the solvent front

üëÅÔ∏è Observations

  • ‚Ä¢ Count and record the number of spots
  • ‚Ä¢ Measure distances traveled by each spot and the solvent

‚úÖ Conclusion

  • ‚Ä¢ One spot = pure substance
  • ‚Ä¢ Multiple spots = impure
  • ‚Ä¢ Compare distances with pure substance to identify components
