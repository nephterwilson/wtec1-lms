The Portuguese Factor in East and Central African Trade

Exploring the Impact of European Arrival on Pre-Colonial Trade Networks

1. How the Portuguese Came into Contact with the East Coast of Africa

The Age of Exploration

  • Portugal is located in Europe, south-west of Spain
  • Portuguese were searching for a sea route to India to access the lucrative spice trade
  • They found the East African coast (Land of Zanj) around 1497
  • Vasco da Gama was the first Portuguese explorer to arrive on the East African coast

What Vasco da Gama Found

  • He saw rich trade in gold and other valuable commodities
  • Recognized the economic potential of the region
  • Wanted Portugal to control this trade for economic dominance
  • Portuguese settled at Sofala and Mozambique Island as key trading bases

2. Organisation of the Gold Trade

The Gold Trade Chain

Step 1: Mining
Local communities mined gold using simple traditional tools and techniques
Step 2: Royal Collection
Gold was given to the king (such as the Mwenemutapa) as tribute
Step 3: Distribution to Middlemen
The king sold gold to middlemen who operated in the interior
Step 4: Coastal Trade
Middlemen sold gold to Arabs and Portuguese traders at the coast
Step 5: Exchange
Gold was exchanged for cloth, beads, guns, wine, and other foreign goods

Portuguese Trading Infrastructure

  • Portuguese built trading posts called Feiras inland to facilitate trade
  • The Mwenemutapa received foreign goods from Portuguese traders
  • The king distributed these goods to his people, strengthening his political power
  • This system created dependency on European trade goods

3. Arab-Portuguese Conflict in the Land of Zanj

The Struggle for Trade Dominance

Both Arabs and Portuguese wanted to control the lucrative gold trade on the East African coast, leading to violent conflicts.

Arabs

  • Fought among themselves
  • Had poor weapons
  • Lacked unity
  • Lost many coastal towns
VS

Portuguese

  • Strong weapons
  • Well-trained soldiers
  • Naval superiority
  • Conquered Arab towns

Portuguese Military Campaigns

  • Portuguese attacked Arab towns like Kilwa, Mombasa, and Sofala
  • Arabs were weakened because they fought each other instead of uniting
  • Arabs had inferior weapons compared to Portuguese cannons and firearms
  • Portuguese had strong weapons and well-trained soldiers
  • Arabs lost many towns to Portuguese conquest by the early 1500s

The Turning Point:

  • Portuguese installed a puppet king named Mavura on the Mwenemutapa throne
  • Mavura signed away his kingdom's sovereignty
  • Portuguese gained full control of trade, land, and mining rights
  • The Mwenemutapa kingdom became a Portuguese vassal state
  • Marked the beginning of colonial domination in the region
  • Local rulers lost independence and became dependent on Portuguese support
