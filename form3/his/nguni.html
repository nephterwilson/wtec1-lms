MSCE History Notes: The Ngoni

Central African History - Malawi Secondary Certificate Examination

1BACKGROUND OF THE NGONI

Meaning of Mfecane

Mfecane means "crushing" or "scattering."
  • The Ngoni migration came as a result of a period called Mfecane.
  • It refers to a time of political instability, wars, and migrations that affected the Nguni-speaking people of Natal, South Africa between 1816 and 1819.

Causes of Mfecane

  • Tribal rivalries among the Nguni.
  • Ambition for power and control over land and cattle.
  • Rise of powerful leaders such as Shaka Zulu, Zwide, and Dingiswayo.

Major Nguni Groups

Group Leader
Ndwandwe Zwide

These groups fought for control of the Nguni region.

2THE RISE OF SHAKA ZULU

Shaka's Leadership

  • Became chief of the Zulu after the death of Dingiswayo.
  • Turned the Zulu into a strong and disciplined army.
  • His leadership marked the beginning of large-scale migrations in Southern Africa.

Shaka's Military Innovations

Important Military Reforms:

  1. Short stabbing spear (Assegai) â€“ used for close combat instead of throwing.
  2. Large cowhide shields â€“ protected soldiers from neck to feet.
  3. "Cow-horn" formation â€“ army divided into chest (centre) and horns (flanks) to surround enemies.
  4. Surprise attacks at dawn and dusk.
  5. Use of spies, runners, and smoke signals to get information quickly.

Defeat of Zwide

  • After Dingiswayo's death, Shaka resisted Zwide's attacks.
  • The decisive battle took place at Mhlatuze River (1819) where Zwide was defeated.
  • Shaka became the master of the region.

Results of Mfecane

  • Many weaker groups fled northwards into Central Africa.
  • Among these were the Ndebele, Kololo, and Ngoni, who formed new kingdoms in Central and Southern Africa.

3MIGRATION OF THE JERE NGONI

Leadership Under Zwangendaba

  • Origin: Northern Zululand
  • Leader: Zwangendaba, son of Hlatshayo (former commander under Zwide)
  • Composition: Swazi, Tsonga, Nsenga, and Karanga people who were assimilated

Reasons for Migration

  1. Instability caused by Shaka's wars
  2. Desire to establish their own kingdom
  3. Shortage of land and resources
  4. Quest for cattle and safety
  5. Escape from powerful rulers in South Africa

4SUCCESSION CRISIS AFTER ZWANGENDABA'S DEATH

Crisis: A serious conflict or struggle for power that threatens stability.

The Succession Struggle (1848)

  • Zwangendaba died at Mapupo in 1848
  • Chosen heir: M'mbelwa, son of a younger wife
  • Opposition: Elders supported Mpezeni, the eldest son
  • Regent appointed: Ntabeni (Zwangendaba's brother) became temporary ruler
  • When Ntabeni declared Mpezeni as heir, M'mbelwa's supporters rebelled
  • This led to the division of the Ngoni into separate groups

Outcome of the Crisis

  • M'mbelwa's supporters moved south to Malawi, establishing the kingdom at Mzimba
  • The Ngoni split into at least five major groups, each settling in different regions

6MZIMBA: THE MAIN NGONI KINGDOM

Why Mzimba Was Chosen

  1. Rich grazing land free from tsetse flies
  2. Enough water and fertile soil
  3. Room for settlement and farming

Mzimba Under the British

  • Missionaries advised Harry Johnston not to attack Ngoni
  • Followed diplomatic approach with peaceful negotiations
  • 24 October 1904: Mzimba became part of British Protectorate
  • First British resident: Hector Macdonald
  • Ngoni agreed to stop raids and pay taxes
