Anglican Universities Unite

Established in 1857 by Anglican members from Oxford, Cambridge, and Durham universities in response to Livingstone's passionate appeal. Led by Bishop Charles Fredrick Mackenzie, they reached the Zambezi River in 1861.

Move to Zanzibar (1864)

New Leadership: Bishop George Tozer took command of the mission.

Reasons for Relocation:

  • Sparse population among the Kololo people
  • Strong local resistance to missionary work
  • Unsuitable conditions for mission expansion

Symbolic Location: Built a cathedral on a former slave market site.

Return to Malawi (1885)

Leadership: William Percival Johnstone led the return.

Headquarters: Established at Likoma Island on Lake Malawi.

Charles Jansen: Steam boat that linked stations across the lake and served as a floating teacher training college.

St. Peter's Cathedral: Major landmark built on Likoma.

Expansion: Mission work spread to Nkhotakota, Malindi, Mangochi, and Zomba.

Ngoni (Njuyu) Mission (1882)

Challenge: Gradual pacification of the militaristic Ngoni people.

Leadership: Dr. Robert Laws, assisted by William Ntusane Koyi, Dr. Walter Elmslie, and James Sutherland.

First Converts: Mawelera Tembo and Chitezi Tembo (Nsenga tribe, not Ngoni).

Additional Missions:

  • Ekwendeni (1889)
  • Hora Mountain (1893)

Slow Progress: By 1892, only 11 converts in Ngoniland.

Notable Graduates & Leaders

Livingstonia Mission produced many of Malawi's future leaders who would shape the nation's politics and church:

  • Dr. Hastings Kamuzu Banda - First President of Malawi

  • Charles Chidongo Chinula - Religious leader

Blantyre Mission (1876)

Established Church of Scotland - Named after Livingstone's hometown

Site chosen in Mudi Valley with approval from Yao chief Kapeni. The mission initially involved 5 artisans and a doctor, but notably no clergy.

Challenges

Slave Trade & Raids: Ongoing slave trade and Ngoni raids constantly disrupted missionary work.

Achievements

Key Leaders: Dr. David Clement Scott (1881) and Rev. Alexander Heatherwick (1889) transformed the mission.
