New Leadership: Bishop George Tozer took command of the mission.

Return to Malawi (1885)

Leadership: William Percival Johnstone led the return.

Headquarters: Established at Likoma Island on Lake Malawi.

St. Peter's Cathedral: Major landmark built on Likoma.

Expansion: Mission work spread to Nkhotakota, Malindi, Mangochi, and Zomba.

Blantyre Mission (1876)

Established Church of Scotland - Named after Livingstone's hometown

Site chosen in Mudi Valley with approval from Yao chief Kapeni. The mission initially involved 5 artisans and a doctor, but notably no clergy.

Challenges

Formation of CCAP

By the 1920s, the three major Scottish and Dutch missions united to form the Church of Central Africa Presbyterian (CCAP). Livingstonia Mission (north), Blantyre Mission (south), and DRCM (central) became the three synods of this unified church, creating a powerful Presbyterian presence that continues to shape Malawi today.

"The missionary factor transformed Malawi forever. While bringing both positive changes and cultural disruption, their work laid foundations for modern education, healthcare, and religious life in the nation."

The missionary period remains one of the most significant and controversial eras in Malawian history. While missionaries brought education, healthcare, and ended the slave trade, they also facilitated colonialism and eroded traditional cultures. Understanding both the positive contributions and negative consequences helps us appreciate the complex legacy that continues to shape Malawi today.
