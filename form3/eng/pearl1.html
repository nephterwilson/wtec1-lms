Character Summary

Kino is a complex character who demonstrates both admirable and flawed traits throughout "The Pearl." His journey from a simple pearl diver to a man consumed by ambition and paranoia showcases Steinbeck's exploration of how material wealth can corrupt even the most well-intentioned individuals.

The pearl, which initially represents hope and opportunity, becomes a symbol of greed, violence, and tragedy. Kino's character arc serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of materialism and the importance of recognizing what truly matters in life.
