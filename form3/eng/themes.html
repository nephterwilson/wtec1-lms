Themes in The Pearl

(I) The Theme of Love

→ Juana shows her love to Coyotito by sucking the bite.

→ Juna brings out his wife Appolonia nicely to Kino's house since they wear the life.

→ Juana massages Kino's bruised forehead.

→ Juana found promises to NEVER his brother Kino.

→ Juana refuses to part ways with Kino during their trying moments.

→ Juana remains vigilant or awake to guarding Kino and Coyotito as Kino falls asleep.

(II) The Theme of Poverty

→ Kino's family has no money to pay the Doctor to treat Coyotito.

→ The family of Kino has an old canoe which they can afford or use their labour.

→ Having a canoe in La Paz is a symbol of survival.

→ Kino's community lives in brush houses as opposed to the Doctors' building.

→ Kino and his family do not have decent clothes.

→ All along Kino has not wedded in church due to lack of money.

→ Kino desires to have a rifle but was failing to buy it due to lack of money.

→ Dependency of fishing (on fishing) and Pearl searching is a sign of poverty.

→ Kino and his family sleep on unflored house and this is shown when he digs and buries the Pearl in the ground.

(III) The Theme of Wealth

→ Wealth produces life with positive creative and negative results.

→ Wealth makes one to get the needs of the family.

→ Wealth produces the force of attraction
a. Doctor and Priest visit Kino

→ Wealth changes one's line of thoughts
a. Kino wants to buy a rifle to protect himself
The moment he becomes rich.

→ Wealth makes trouble to an individual
a. Kino house is set a blaze
a. Kino's canoe is destroyed
a. Kino's son is shot dead.

→ Wealth makes people to plan evil against an individual
a. Everyone wants to cheat Kino in order to get the Pearl.

(IV) The Theme of Good versus Evil

→ The family is good because it Kino's comfort during trying moments, but the same family is evil when it brings problems due to stubbornness of one person.

→ The Pearl is good when it changes one's state but the same Pearl is evil when it brings enemies and robs one's peace as it's evil, but, Kino.

→ Love is good as it brings consolation and is the best medicine during hard time, but the same love is evil when it is excessive and stops one from thinking.
a. Had Juana listened to her husband, Coyotito wouldn't
be killed but Juana's love to Kino killed Coyotito.

(V) Male Chauvinism (The Theme)

→ Kino being a man automatically becomes the head of the family and Juana has to respect the decisions made by Kino without question or denial.

→ Appolonia doesn't question the decisions made by Juan Tomas.

→ By being a man, Kino becomes the spokesperson of the family, Juan Tomas and not Appolonia say little if any.

(VI) The Theme of The Importance of The Family

→ Juana's presence gives Kino walieth (wealth) and raises him when he is attacked.

→ Kino wants to marry in Church when he sells his Pearl.

→ Kino is accompanied by his family when going to sell the Pearl.

→ Juan and Appolinia seem to enjoy Kino's protection as the head of the family.

→ Juana's and Appolinia's sympathize with Kino following what is happening to him.
a. He delivers his brother not to be cheated by local buyers.

→ She offers them an accommodation when their house is on fire.

→ The family doubted Kino hence tells us the importance of the family.

(VII) The theme of Lack of Wisdom

→ Kino doesn't listen to the advice that is coming from his wife to do away with the Pearl lest it has the potential to destroying them.

→ Juana's (clinging) to have the Pearl leads to the burning of the house, death of the canoe and death of Coyotito.

→ Juana believes that if Kino sells the Pearl he will buy time for himself, but he refuses.

→ Kino beats up his wife for trying to throw the Pearl back to the sea.

→ Kino doesn't believe the advice of his elder brother, Juan Tomas, that there is the devil in the Pearl and has to be sold or thrown into the sea to truce on the devil.

→ Kino casts the Pearl back into the sea after the death of Coyotito.

(VIII) The Theme of Obsession

→ Kino would like to pay the Doctor for Coyotito's treatment and he has one valuable Pearl.

→ Kino wants a Pearl in order to get one with great value.

→ Kino considers the Pearl as the fulfillment of his dreams.

→ Kino holds a man when he is attacked for self-defence.

→ Kino viciously beats up his wife when she tries to throw the Pearl back into the sea.

→ Kino clings to the Pearl until the death of his son Coyotito.

→ Kino claims that the Pearl has become his soul.

→ Kino kills three men for attempting to get the Pearl from him on his way to the Capital.

(IX) The Theme Curiosity

→ Kino becomes so curious when he gets the Pearl of the world so he would get what he failed to get all along.

→ Juana is so curious when Kino comes out of the sea with the most valuable oyster containing a Pearl.

→ Kino becomes so curious with the fast-speaking of the news of his getting of the Pearl
a. May be then, Coyolita boys can scramble and dart to tell it.

→ The Priest becomes so curious on the news of Kino's getting a valuable Pearl
a. Though if is evening, he pays Kino a visit.

→ The Doctor is curious about that Kino got the Pearl of the world
a. He wants to know where the Pearl is hidden
a. He claims to know the size of the Pearl
a. He suggests to keep the Pearl safely.

→ The Pearl buyers are so anxious to see Kino's Pearl until his children see whole town
a. They can with the lowest price offer.

→ The beggars become so curious to Kino's sudden change of status from ruvely to prosperity
a. They are concerned about almost quick and trace bids
suddenly becomes rich.

→ The trackers and thieves become so curious about Kino's wealth and plan several means of how to rob him.

(X) The Theme of Optimism

→ Kino is quite confident that the Pearl is going to change his economic status from poor to rich.

→ The Doctor is quite confident that Kino will give him the Pearl when he demands for it.

→ The Doctor thinks that after selling the Pearl, Kino will pay him handsomely.

→ The Priest thinks that the Church and him will greatly benefit from Kino's fortunes.

→ The buyers are quite confident that the seller will sell their Pearls to them despite the lower prices offered.

(XI) The Theme of Exploitation

→ The Pearl buyers want to buy the Pearls at very low prices taking advantage of the fact that divers are poor.

→ The Priest wants the sales from the Pearl to assist him and the church, taking advantage of the Christian life of Kino and Juana.

→ The Doctor wants to keep Kino's Pearl, taking advantage of his poverty.

(XII) The Theme of Corruption

→ Corruption has fueled greed — clearly seen in the lives of some individuals and the society at large.

from a china cup while refuse to treat Coyotito
