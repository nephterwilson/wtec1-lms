UNIT 2: Chemical Properties of Soil

Understanding Soil Fertility and Nutrient Dynamics

Introduction

The chemical properties of soil are crucial in determining its fertility and suitability for crop production. These properties affect nutrient availability, pH balance, salinity, and the ability to retain essential elements for plant growth.

1. Nutrient Status

The nutrient status refers to the soil's ability to support fertility, which determines soil productivity. More nutrients result in higher fertility, while less nutrient content leads to lower fertility.

Macronutrients

Essential nutrients required by plants in large quantities

Factors Affecting Nutrient Status

Parent Material

Soils from nutrient-poor rocks have fewer nutrients

Soil pH

pH influences nutrient solubility and availability

Leaching

Rainfall and irrigation can wash away nutrients

Soil Erosion

Removes nutrient-rich topsoil

Crop Removal

Harvesting and deforestation deplete nutrients

Plant Uptake

Roots absorb essential elements

Factors Affecting Soil pH

Parent Material

Limestone (basic) or quartz-rich sandstone (acidic) influences pH

Fertilizer Use

Certain fertilizers produce hydrogen ions altering pH

Leaching

High rainfall leaches basic ions affecting pH

Vegetation

Plants like conifers produce organic acids lowering pH

Ways to Modify Soil pH

Application of agricultural lime to reduce acidity

Use of organic manure

Applying inorganic fertilizers such as urea or urea phosphate

Use of acidic fertilizers (e.g., ammonium sulfate) over multiple planting cycles

3. Salinity

Soil salinity refers to the salt content present in the soil. High salt accumulation can severely impact crop production.

Effects of High Salinity

Soil Degradation

Salt buildup damages soil structure

Poor Crop Yields

Reduced productivity and harvest

Plant Death

Dehydration and wilting occur

Causes of Salt Build-up

Irrigation

Improper water management

Parent Rock Material

Natural mineral composition

Certain Fertilizers

Excessive chemical application

Compost and Manure

Organic matter with high salt content

Slow or Excessive Water Application

Poor drainage and water management practices
