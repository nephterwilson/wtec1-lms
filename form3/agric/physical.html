Physical Properties of Soil

Understanding Soil for Successful Crop Production

Physical properties of soil are critical factors that determine agricultural suitability. Understanding these properties enables informed decisions about crop production and land management.

1 Soil Texture

The relative proportions of different-sized soil particles that influence moisture and nutrient retention.

Clay

Finest particles, high water retention

Sand

Coarse particles, good drainage

Loam

Balanced mixture, ideal for crops

2 Soil Colour

An observable indicator of soil composition and fertility influenced by organic matter, moisture, and parent material.

Red/Brown

Well-drained soil

Grey

Generally infertile

Black/Dark Brown

High organic matter content

Yellow/White/Grey

Specific mineral presence

3 Soil Temperature

The degree of hotness or coldness affecting biological, physical, and chemical soil processes.

Influences:

Seed germination
Microbial activity
Flowering & root growth
Nutrient uptake rate

4 Soil Depth

Thickness of the topsoil layer, critical for determining suitable crop types.

Deep Soils

Suitable for deep-rooted crops with better water retention

Shallow Soils

Better for shallow-rooted crops with limited water storage

5 Soil Consistency

The ease with which soil can be broken down, related to moisture content.

Moist Soil

Loose, friable, or firm consistency

Wet Soil

Classified by stickiness and plasticity levels

Dry Soil

Soft and loose OR hard and resistant

6 Soil Structure

Arrangement of soil particles into aggregates (peds) allowing water and air movement.

Granular

Topsoil, porous, ideal for crops

Blocky

Dry soils, B-horizon

Crumb

Humus and clay mixture

Prismatic

Vertical columns, B-horizon

Columnar

Salt caps, arid areas

Platy

Horizontal plates, clay soils

7 Soil Porosity

Number of pore spaces within soil occupied by air and water.
