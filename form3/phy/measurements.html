Global Standardization

Measurements in science use the International System of Units (SI Units). This system ensures standardization in measuring physical quantities globally, enabling scientists worldwide to communicate and collaborate effectively.

Consistency

SI units provide a consistent method of measurement across all scientific disciplines and countries.

Unique Symbols

Each quantity has a base unit with a unique symbol recognized internationally.

üí°

Why SI Units Matter

Before the SI system, different countries and regions used various measurement systems, leading to confusion and errors. The SI system eliminates these problems by providing a universal language for measurement. Whether you're in Tokyo, New York, or London, a metre is always a metre, and a kilogram is always a kilogram.

1

Instrument Selection

Question:

State when you would use:

(a) Vernier calipers

(b) Micrometer screw gauge

Answer:

(a) Vernier calipers: When measuring internal or external dimensions requiring accuracy to 0.01 cm, such as the diameter of a pipe or the width of a small object.

(b) Micrometer screw gauge: When measuring very small dimensions requiring accuracy to 0.001 cm, such as wire thickness or the diameter of a ball bearing.

2

Using Instruments

Question:

Explain how to use a Vernier caliper correctly.

Answer:

1. Close the jaws gently on the object to be measured

2. Read the main scale at the zero mark of the Vernier scale

3. Find the Vernier line that aligns with a main scale line

4. Add the two readings together for the final measurement

3

Measuring Thickness

Question:

Describe how Liz can measure her finger thickness accurately.

Answer:

Method 1 (Vernier calipers): Liz should place her finger between the external jaws of the Vernier calipers, ensuring gentle pressure without compressing the finger. Read the measurement as described above.

Method 2 (Micrometer): For greater accuracy, place the finger between the anvil and spindle of a micrometer screw gauge, taking care not to apply excessive pressure.
