Understanding Molecular Motion

The Kinetic Theory explains the behavior of matter at the molecular level. Even though we cannot see individual molecules, they are constantly in motion - vibrating, rotating, and moving through space.

The amount and type of motion determines whether matter exists as a solid, liquid, or gas. Understanding this theory helps us explain phenomena like heating, cooling, expansion, and diffusion.
