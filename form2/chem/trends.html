Periodic Trends

Atomic Radius

The distance from the nucleus to the outermost electrons.

↓ Increases down a group (more energy levels)
→ Decreases across a period (stronger nuclear charge)

Example: Group 1

Li < Na < K (increasing atomic radius)

Ionization Energy

Energy required to remove an outermost electron.

↓ Decreases down a group (electrons further from nucleus)
→ Increases across a period (stronger attraction)

Inverse Relationship

As atomic radius increases, ionization energy decreases

Electronegativity

Ability to attract shared electrons in a bond.

↓ Decreases down a group
→ Increases across a period

Electron Affinity

Energy released when an atom gains an electron.

↓ Generally decreases down a group
→ Generally increases across a period

Group I - Alkali Metals

Li

Lithium

Atomic Number: 3

Scarlet flame color

Na

Sodium

Atomic Number: 11

Yellow flame color

K

Potassium

Atomic Number: 19

Lilac flame color

Physical Properties

  • Soft metals (can be cut with knife)
  • Low melting and boiling points
  • Shiny when freshly cut
  • Low density (float on water)
  • Good conductors of heat and electricity

Chemical Properties

  • Highly reactive (stored in oil)
  • Form +1 ions easily
  • React vigorously with water
  • Tarnish quickly in air
  • Reactivity increases down the group

Group II - Alkaline Earth Metals

Be

Beryllium

Atomic Number: 4

Mg

Magnesium

Atomic Number: 12

Ca

Calcium

Atomic Number: 20

Sr

Strontium

Atomic Number: 38

Key Characteristics

  • Form +2 ions
  • Higher melting points than alkali metals
  • Less reactive than Group I
  • Reactivity increases down the group

Common Uses

  • Mg: Lightweight alloys, pharmaceuticals
  • Ca: Cement production, antacids
  • Be: X-ray applications, aerospace

  • • Toothpaste (prevents decay)

  • • Refrigeration systems

Chlorine Uses

  • • Water treatment
  • • PVC production
  • • Bleaching agents

Bromine Uses

  • • Photography
  • • Flame retardants
  • • Anti-knock agents

Iodine Uses

  • • Medical disinfectant
  • • Table salt additive
  • • Starch testing

Group VIII - Noble Gases

He

Helium

Balloons, cooling

Ne

Neon

Advertising signs

Ar

Argon

Light bulbs, welding

Unique Properties

Fully filled outer electron shells
Very unreactive (inert)
Exist as monoatomic gases
Do not conduct electricity
Atomic size increases down group
Colorless and odorless

Experiment 1.3: Alkali Metals with Water

Aim: Investigate reactions of Li, Na, K with water

🧪 Materials: Glass trough, tongs, universal indicator
⚠️ Safety: Use tongs, observe from distance

Experiment 1.4: Alkali Metals with Chlorine

Aim: Observe reactions of Li, Na with chlorine gas

🧪 Materials: Deflagrating spoon, chlorine jars
⚠️ Safety: Use fume cupboard, avoid K reaction

Experiment 1.5: Alkaline Earth with Air

Aim: Compare Mg and Ca reactions with oxygen

🧪 Materials: Mg ribbon, Ca granules, Bunsen burner
⚠️ Safety: Don't look directly at burning Mg

Halogen Reactivity Test

Aim: Compare halogen reactivity with metals

🧪 Materials: Various metals, halogen solutions
⚠️ Safety: Handle halogens with care
